Task

When comparing MusicXML with MEI, I found that overall, the clear difference is in how the documents are structured. Firstly, MusicXML seems to have longer files as they are more structured and easier to understand without the need for special documentation. For example elements are written in whole e.g octave and duration whereas in correspondence to MEI, these elements are shortened to oct and dur. Secondly, the format for the representation of pitch is slightly different. In MusicXML, pitch is represented using the octave element nested within the pitch element, whereas in MEI, pitch is represented using the pname attribute. Lastly, in MusicXML, articulations are nested under notations as explicit elements such as staccato whereas in MEI, they are directly nested in the note element as artic="stacc". These structural differences show that MusicXML is more straightforward making it easy to read and software compatible, whereas MEI allows for more precision and detail making it the better type for more complex music notation.
